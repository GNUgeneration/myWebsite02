The Road Not Taken, by Robert Frost

Two roads diverged in a yellow wood and sorry I could not travel both and be one traveler long I stood and looked down one as far as I could to where it bent around the undergrowth. Then took the other as just as fair and having perhaps the better claim as it was grassy and wanted wear though as for that the passing there had worn them really about the same. And both that morning equally lay in leaves no step had troden black Oh, I kept the first for another day! yet knowing how way leads on to way I doubted if I should ever come back. I shall be telling this with a sigh somewhere ages and ages hence two roads diverged in a wood and I -- I took the one less traveled by and that has made all the difference.
